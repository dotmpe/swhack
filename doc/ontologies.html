Ontologies

An ontology is a precise specification of a vocabulary. It typically consists of the set of vocabulary terms along with declarations about how the terms relate to each other.

For people familiar with object-oriented analysis and design, ontology design is a fairly small step &mdash a step away program mechanics and into purely abstract information structures.

With RDF, we currently have four ontology languages. In order of increasing expressive power:

  • RDFS is quite simple, perhaps too simple.
  • OWL Lite may strike the right balance for many applications.
  • OWL DL offers all the standard Description Logic features
  • OWL Full removes a few restrictions of OWL DL, making reasoning have weaker tractability guarantees. (Obsoleting DAML+OIL.)
